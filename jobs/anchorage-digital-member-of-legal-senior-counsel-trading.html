At Anchorage Digital, we are building the world’s most advanced digital asset platform for institutions to participate in crypto.   Anchorage Digital is a crypto platform that enables institutions to participate in digital assets through custody, staking, trading, governance, settlement, and the industry's leading security infrastructure. Home to Anchorage Digital Bank N.A., the first federally chartered crypto bank in the U.S., Anchorage Digital also serves institutions through Anchorage Digital Singapore, Porto by Anchorage Digital, and other offerings.   The company is funded by leading institutions including Andreessen Horowitz, GIC, Goldman Sachs, KKR, and Visa, with its Series D valuation over $3 billion. Founded in 2017 in San Francisco, California, Anchorage Digital has offices in New York, New York; Porto, Portugal; Singapore; and Sioux Falls, South Dakota. Learn more at anchorage.com, on X @Anchorage, and on LinkedIn.  With the evolving landscape of digital threats and an increasing volume of sophisticated attacks by malicious actors, we have a security policy mandating all new hires complete an in-person onboarding process - no exceptions. While our security and IT logistics require physical presence to protect our infrastructure from sophisticated global threats, this move is equally about our Village culture. The Senior Counsel, Trading is an integral member of the trading solutions business responsible for providing strategic legal advice to bring new products and features to the institutional market. This role requires a builder's mindset and direct involvement from the product discovery stage through establishing, structuring, developing policies, and conducting legal research for regulatory compliance. You will provide legal expertise on market infrastructure, including crypto trading venues, prime brokers, custodians, and payment rails. Responsibilities include structuring, drafting, and negotiating counterparty agreements for prime brokerage features, electronic trading agreements, derivatives, and financing arrangements, while coordinating with internal teams and managing external counsel. Finally, you will provide strategic advice to executive leadership on reputational and regulatory issues and advise on the implementation and ongoing compliance of key relevant regulations.
